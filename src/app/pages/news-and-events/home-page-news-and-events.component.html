WHAT IS PET?

PET is an acronym for polyethylene terephthalate, which is a long-chain polymer belonging to the generic family of polyesters [1]. PET is formed from the intermediates, terephthalic acid (TPA) and ethylene glycol (EG), which are both derived from oil feedstock.

FOOD PACKAGING

In general it can be seen that the desired properties for packaging applications are attained from the intrinsic properties of PET polymer. Therefore additives such as antioxidants, plasticizers, heat or UV stabilizers are not required. .

USE OF RECOVERED PET

Recovered and recycled PET has to comply with all the rules and regulations that apply to virgin materials. The health and safety of the consumer is of prime importance when considering reuse and recycling of plastics, or any materials in food contact uses.
